Solving DGLAP¶

We are solving the DGLAP equations given in x-space by

\[\frac{d}{d\ln(\mu_F^2)} \mathbf{f}(x,\mu_F^2) = \int\limits_x^1\!\frac{dy}{y}\, \mathbf{P}(x/y) \cdot \mathbf{f}(y,\mu_F^2)\]

with \(\mathbf P\) the Altarelli-Parisi splitting functions (see pQCD ingredients). In Mellin space the DGLAP equations are just differential equations:

\[\frac{d}{d\ln(\mu_F^2)} \tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,\mu_F^2) = -\tilde \gamma(N) \cdot \tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,\mu_F^2)\]

(Note the additional minus in the definition for \(\tilde \gamma\)).

We change the evolution variable to \(t(\mu_F^2) = \ln(1/a_s(\mu_F^2))\) and then the differential equations to solve are given by

\[\frac{d}{dt} \tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,t) = \frac{d\ln(\mu_F^2)}{dt} \cdot \frac{d \tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,\mu_F^2)}{d\ln(\mu_F^2)} = \frac{a_s(\mu_F^2)}{\beta(a_s(\mu_F^2))} \cdot \tilde \gamma(N) \cdot \tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,t)\]

This transformation makes the equations explicitly independent of the running coupling at LO.

The solution can be written in terms of an evolution kernel operator \(\mathbf E\) (represented by PhysicalOperator)

\[\tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,t_1) = \tilde{\mathbf{E}}(t_1 \leftarrow t_0) \cdot \tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,t_0)\]

Using Interpolation on both the inital and final PDF, we can then discretize the evolution kernel operator and define \(\hat{\mathbf{E}}\) by

\[\hat{\mathbf{E}}_{k,j}(t_1\leftarrow t_0) = \mathcal{M}^{-1}\left[\tilde{\mathbf{E}}(t_1 \leftarrow t_0)\tilde p_j(N)\right](x_k)\]

Now, we can write the solution to DGLAP in a true matrix operator scheme and find

\[\mathbf{f}(x_k,t_1) = \hat{\mathbf{E}}_{k,j}(t_1\leftarrow t_0) \mathbf{f}(x_j,t_0)\]

In the non-singlet sector the equations decouple and we do not need to worry about matrices.

The issue of matching conditions when crossing flavor thresholds is dicussed in a seperate document

Leading Order¶

Non-Singlet Evolution¶

We find

\[\frac{d}{dt} \tilde f_{ns}^{(0)}(t) = -\frac{1}{\beta_0} \cdot \tilde \gamma_{ns}^{(0)}(N) \cdot \tilde f_{ns}^{(0)}(t)\]

with \(\tilde \gamma_{ns}^{(0)} = \tilde\gamma_{ns,+}^{(0)} = \tilde\gamma_{ns,-}^{(0)} = \tilde\gamma_{ns,v}^{(0)}\).

The evolution kernel operator is then given by a simple exponential [Vog05]

\[\tilde E^{(0)}_{ns}(t_1\leftarrow t_0) = \exp(-(t_1-t_0) \tilde \gamma_{ns}^{(0)}(N)/\beta_0 )\]

Singlet Evolution¶

We find

\[\frac{d}{dt} \dSV{0}{t} = -\frac{1}{\beta_0} \cdot \tilde{\gamma}_S^{(0)} \cdot \dSV{0}{t}\]

with

\[\begin{split}\tilde{\gamma}_S^{(0)} = \begin{pmatrix} \tilde \gamma_{qq}^{(0)} & \tilde \gamma_{qg}^{(0)}\\ \tilde \gamma_{gq}^{(0)} & \tilde \gamma_{gg}^{(0)} \end{pmatrix}\end{split}\]

In order to expontiate this matrix, we decompose it \(\tilde \gamma_S^{(0)} = \lambda_+ \tilde {\bf e}_+ + \lambda_- \tilde {\bf e}_-\) with the eigenvalues \(\lambda_{\pm}\) and the projectors \(\tilde e_{\pm}\) given by [Vog05]

\[\begin{split}\lambda_{\pm} &= \frac 1 2 \left( \gamma_{qq}^{(0)} + \gamma_{gg}^{(0)} + \pm \sqrt{(\gamma_{qq}^{(0)}-\gamma_{gg}^{(0)})^2 + 4\gamma_{qg}^{(0)}\gamma_{gq}^{(0)}} \right)\\ \tilde {\bf e}_{\pm} &= \frac{1}{\lambda_{\pm} - \lambda_{\mp}} \left( \tilde \gamma_S^{(0)} - \lambda_{\mp} \mathbf I \right)\end{split}\]

with \(\mathbf I\) the identity matrix in flavour space. The projectors obey the usual properties, i.e.

\[\tilde {\bf e}_{\pm} \cdot \tilde {\bf e}_{\pm} = \tilde {\bf e}_{\pm} \qquad \tilde {\bf e}_{\pm} \cdot \tilde {\bf e}_{\mp} = 0\]

and thus the expontiation becomes easier again.

The evolution kernel operator is then given by

\[\tilde{\mathbf{E}}^{(0)}_{S}(t_1\leftarrow t_0) = \tilde{\mathbf{e}}_-\exp(-(t_1-t_0) \lambda_{-}(N)/\beta_0 ) + \tilde{\mathbf{e}}_+\exp(-(t_1-t_0) \lambda_{+}(N)/\beta_0 )\]
