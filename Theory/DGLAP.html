Solving DGLAPÂ¶

We are solving the DGLAP equations given in x-space by

\[\frac{d}{d\ln(\mu_F^2)} \mathbf{f}(x,\mu_F^2) = \int\limits_x^1\!\frac{dy}{y}\, \mathbf{P}(x/y,a_s) \cdot \mathbf{f}(y,\mu_F^2)\]

with \(\mathbf P\) the Altarelli-Parisi splitting functions (see pQCD ingredients). In Mellin space the DGLAP equations are just differential equations:

\[\frac{d}{d\ln(\mu_F^2)} \tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,\mu_F^2) = -\gamma(N,a_s) \cdot \tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,\mu_F^2)\]

(Note the additional minus in the definition for \(\gamma\)).

We change the evolution variable to (monotonic) Strong Coupling \(a_s(\mu_F^2)\) and the equations to solve become

\[\frac{d}{da_s} \tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,a_s) = \frac{d\ln(\mu_F^2)}{da_s} \cdot \frac{d \tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,\mu_F^2)}{d\ln(\mu_F^2)} = -\frac{1}{\beta(a_s)} \cdot \gamma(N,a_s) \cdot \tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,a_s)\]

The (formal) solution can then be written in terms of an evolution kernel operator (EKO) \(\mathbf E\)

\[\begin{split}\tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,a_s^1) &= \tilde{\mathbf{E}}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) \cdot \tilde{\mathbf{f}}(N,a_s^0)\\ \tilde{\mathbf{E}}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) &= \exp\left[-\int\limits_{a_s^0}^{a_s^1} \frac{\gamma(N,a_s)}{\beta(a_s)} da_s \right]\end{split}\]

Using Interpolation on both the inital and final PDF, we can then discretize the EKO in x-space and define \(\hat{\mathbf{E}}_{k,j}\) (represented by PhysicalOperator) by

\[\hat{\mathbf{E}}_{k,j}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) = \mathcal{M}^{-1}\left[\tilde{\mathbf{E}}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0)\tilde p_j(N)\right](x_k)\]

Now, we can write the solution to DGLAP in a true matrix operator scheme and find

\[\mathbf{f}(x_k,a_s^1) = \hat{\mathbf{E}}_{k,j}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) \mathbf{f}(x_j,a_s^0)\]

In the non-singlet sector the equations decouple and we do not need to worry about matrices.

The issue of matching conditions when crossing flavor thresholds is dicussed in a seperate document

LO Non-Singlet EvolutionÂ¶

We find

\[\frac{d}{da_s} \tilde f_{ns}^{(0)}(a_s) = \frac{\gamma_{ns}^{(0)}}{\beta_0 a_s} \cdot \tilde f_{ns}^{(0)}(a_s)\]

with \(\gamma_{ns}^{(0)} = \gamma_{ns,+}^{(0)} = \gamma_{ns,-}^{(0)} = \gamma_{ns,v}^{(0)} = \gamma_{qq}^{(0)}\).

The EKO is then given by a simple exponential [Vog05]

\[\tilde E^{(0)}_{ns}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) = \exp\left[\ln(a_s^1/a_s^0) \gamma_{ns}^{(0)}/\beta_0 \right]\]

LO Singlet EvolutionÂ¶

We find

\[\frac{d}{da_s} \dSV{0}{a_s} = \frac{\mathbf R_0}{a_s} \cdot \dSV{0}{a_s}\]

with

\[\begin{split}\mathbf R_0 = \frac{\gamma_S^{(0)}}{\beta_0}\,, \qquad \gamma_S^{(0)} = \begin{pmatrix} \gamma_{qq}^{(0)} & \gamma_{qg}^{(0)}\\ \gamma_{gq}^{(0)} & \gamma_{gg}^{(0)} \end{pmatrix}\end{split}\]

In order to exponentiate this matrix, we decompose it \(\mathbf R_0 = \lambda_+ {\mathbf e}_+ + \lambda_- {\mathbf e}_-\) with the eigenvalues \(\lambda_{\pm}\) and the projectors \(\mathbf e_{\pm}\) given by [Vog05]

\[\begin{split}\lambda_{\pm} &= \frac 1 {2\beta_0} \left( \gamma_{qq}^{(0)} + \gamma_{gg}^{(0)} \pm \sqrt{(\gamma_{qq}^{(0)}-\gamma_{gg}^{(0)})^2 + 4\gamma_{qg}^{(0)}\gamma_{gq}^{(0)}} \right)\\ {\mathbf e}_{\pm} &= \frac{1}{\lambda_{\pm} - \lambda_{\mp}} \left( \mathbf R_0 - \lambda_{\mp} \mathbf I \right)\end{split}\]

with \(\mathbf I\) the 2x2 identity matrix in flavour space. The projectors obey the usual properties, i.e.

\[{\mathbf e}_{\pm} \cdot {\mathbf e}_{\pm} = {\mathbf e}_{\pm}\,,\quad {\mathbf e}_{\pm} \cdot {\mathbf e}_{\mp} = 0\,,\quad \ep + \em = \mathbf I\]

and thus the exponentiation becomes easier again.

The EKO is then given by

\[\ESk{0}{a_s^1}{a_s^0} = \em \exp\left[\ln(a_s^1/a_s^0) \lambda_{-} \right] + \ep \exp\left[\ln(a_s^1/a_s^0) \lambda_{+} \right]\]

NLO Non-Singlet EvolutionÂ¶

We find

\[\frac{d}{da_s} \tilde f_{ns}^{(1)}(a_s) = \frac{\gamma_{ns}^{(0)} a_s + \gamma_{ns}^{(1)} a_s^2}{\beta_0 a_s^2 + \beta_1 a_s^3} \cdot \tilde f_{ns}^{(1)}(a_s)\]

with \(\gamma_{ns} \in \{\gamma_{ns,+},\gamma_{ns,-}=\gamma_{ns,v}\}\).

To solve this equation we provide the same 4 strategies as [Vog05] :

  • the exact solution to the EKO is given by the exponentials: [RA98][Vog05]

\[\begin{split}\tilde E^{(1),\text{exact}}_{ns}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) &= \tilde E^{(0)}_{ns}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) \exp\left[\ln\left(\frac{1+a_s^1 b_1}{1 + a_s^0 b_1}\right)\bar \gamma_{ns}^{(1)} \right] \\ \bar \gamma_{ns}^{(1)} &= \frac{\gamma_{ns}^{(1)}}{\beta_1} - \frac{\gamma_{ns}^{(0)}}{\beta_0}\end{split}\]

  • expanding the argument of the exponential of the new term we obtain:

\[\tilde E^{(1),\text{LL}}_{ns}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) = \tilde E^{(0)}_{ns}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) \exp\left[(a_s^1 - a_s^0) b_1 \bar \gamma_{ns}^{(1)} \right]\]

  • expanding the argument of the exponential of the new term we obtain, but keeping the order:

\[\tilde E^{(1),\text{truncated'}}_{ns}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) = \tilde E^{(0)}_{ns}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) \frac{1 + a_s^1 b_1 \bar \gamma_{ns}^{(1)}}{1 + a_s^0 b_1 \bar \gamma_{ns}^{(1)}}\]

  • expanding the whole exponential of the new term we obtain:

\[\tilde E^{(1),\text{truncated}}_{ns}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) = \tilde E^{(0)}_{ns}(a_s^1 \leftarrow a_s^0) \left[1 + (a_s^1 - a_s^0) b_1 \bar \gamma_{ns}^{(1)} \right]\]

NLO Singlet EvolutionÂ¶

We find

\[\frac{d}{da_s} \dSV{1}{a_s} = \frac{\gamma_{S}^{(0)} a_s + \gamma_{S}^{(1)} a_s^2}{\beta_0 a_s^2 + \beta_1 a_s^3} \cdot \dSV{1}{a_s}\]

with \(\gamma_{S}^{(0)} \gamma_{S}^{(1)} \neq \gamma_{S}^{(1)} \gamma_{S}^{(0)}\).

We are seeking for an perturbative solution around the (exact) leading order operator: [Vog05]

\[\frac{d}{da_s} \dSV{1}{a_s} = \frac{\mathbf R (a_s)}{a_s} \cdot \dSV{1}{a_s}\,, \quad \mathbf R (a_s) = \sum\limits_{k=0} a_s^k \mathbf R_{k}\]

where in NLO we find (in addition to the LO operator)

\[\mathbf R_1 = \gamma_{S}^{(1)}/\beta_0 - b_1 \gamma_{S}^{(0)} \quad \text{and} \quad \mathbf R_k = - b_1 \mathbf R_{k-1}\,\text{for}\,k>1\]

Likewise for the solution we set

\[\dSV{1}{a_s^1} = \mathbf U (a_s^1) \ESk{1}{a_s^1}{a_s^0} {\mathbf U}^{-1} (a_s^0) \dSV{1}{a_s^0}\,, \quad \mathbf U (a_s) = \mathbf I + \sum\limits_{k=1} a_s^k \mathbf U_k\]

Inserting this ansatz into the differential equation and sorting by powers of \(a_s\), we obtain a recursive set of commutator relations:

\[\begin{split}[\mathbf U_1, \mathbf R_0] &= \mathbf R_1 - \mathbf U_1\\ [\mathbf U_k, \mathbf R_0] &= \mathbf R_k + \sum\limits_{j=1}^{k-1} \mathbf R_{k-j} \mathbf U_j - k \mathbf U_k = \mathbf{\tilde{R}}_k - k \mathbf U_k\,,k>1\end{split}\]

Multiplying these equations with \(\mathbf e_{\pm}\) from left and right and using the identity

\[\mathbf U_k = \em \mathbf U_k \em + \em \mathbf U_k \ep + \ep \mathbf U_k \em + \ep \mathbf U_k \ep\]

one can obtain the evolution operator coefficients \(\mathbf U_k\) and find:

\[\mathbf U_k = \frac{ \em \mathbf{\tilde{R}}_k \em + \em \mathbf{\tilde{R}}_k \ep } k + \frac{\ep \mathbf{\tilde{R}}_k \em}{r_- - r_+ - k} + \frac{\ep \mathbf{\tilde{R}}_k \ep}{r_+ - r_- -k}\]
