Evolution BasisÂ¶

The evolution flavours are

\[V, V_3, V_8, V_{15}, V_{24}, V_{35}, T_3, T_8, T_{15}, T_{24}, T_{35}, \Sigma, g\]

and they can be seperated into two major classes: the singlet sector, consisting of the singlet distribution \(\Sigma\) and the gluon distribution \(g\), and the non-singlet sector. The non-singlet sector can be again subdivided into three groups: first the full valence distribution \(V\), second the valence-like distributions \(V_3 \ldots V_{35}\), and third the singlet like distributions \(T_3 \ldots T_{35}\). The mapping between the Evolution Basis and the Flavour Basis is given by

\[\begin{split}\Sigma &= \sum\limits_{j} q_j^+\\ V &= \sum\limits_{j} q_j^-\\ V_3 &= u^- - d^-\\ V_8 &= u^- + d^- - 2 s^-\\ V_{15} &= u^- + d^- + s^- - 3 c^-\\ V_{24} &= u^- + d^- + s^- + c^- - 4 b^-\\ V_{35} &= u^- + d^- + s^- + c^- + b^- - 5 t^-\\ T_3 &= u^+ - d^+\\ T_8 &= u^+ + d^+ - 2 s^+\\ T_{15} &= u^+ + d^+ + s^+ - 3 c^+\\ T_{24} &= u^+ + d^+ + s^+ + c^+ - 4 b^+\\ T_{35} &= u^+ + d^+ + s^+ + c^+ + b^+ - 5 t^+\end{split}\]

with

\[q^\pm = q \pm \bar q\]

Note that the associated numbers to the valence-like and singlet-like non-singlet distributions \(k\) follow the common group-theoretical notation \(k = n_f^2 - 1\) where \(n_f\) denotes the incorporated number of quark flavours.

The matching conditions when crossing flavour thresholds are given in Matching Conditions.
